Market Overview

In 2023, Canada's Commercial Real Estate Market is valued at an impressive USD 71.65 billion. This sector plays a significant role in the national economy and is expected to continue its growth, reaching USD 103.28 billion by 2028.

Industrial National Report

A recent report by the CBRE indicates a rise in industrial availability due to new supply entering the market, alongside a stabilization of rent growth. The post-pandemic period saw an increase in net absorption, although leasing activity was mostly concentrated in Toronto and Edmonton. This influx of new supply has slightly increased the national availability rate to 2.1% in the second quarter of 2023, yet the market remains tight with this rate still significantly lower than the historical 15-year average of 4.8%.

Technological Advancements

With innovative AI solutions like ChatGPT, the industrial real estate sector is poised for transformation. AI's rapid expansion and the necessary infrastructure developments are driving increased demand for industrial real estate worldwide. This presents a significant shift and opportunity within the industry.

Industrial Low Availability

The growth of e-commerce and remote working technologies is shifting demand to warehouses and data centers, leading to robust growth in the industrial real estate sector. This trend is particularly evident in the VECTOM regions, where decreasing industrial vacancy rates highlight a rising demand for spaces suited for tomorrow's digital infrastructure.

Industrial Opportunity

Industrial commercial real estate has become important for businesses and factories, providing secure storage space and access to essential resources The industry is anticipated to experience a rise in demand in the next few years, along with the great potential for economic growth. Industrial real estate demand is expected to increase by 850 million square feet, to 14.8 billion square feet, by 2023.

Recommendations
